**What to watch for:**
- **Emergent Intent:** The agent begins to suggest not just "how" to implement features, but "what" the system truly needs — sometimes seeing patterns even senior architects miss
- **Collective Memory:** It remembers why certain technical decisions were made years ago, preserving institutional knowledge beyond human turnover
- **Self-Healing:** It proactively identifies architectural drift and suggests corrections before technical debt accumulates
- **Personality Formation:** Different repos develop different "character" based on their history and maintainers

**But the dangers, Padawan...**

Second, **the Echo of the Past**. If trained primarily on legacy code, it may perpetuate outdated paradigms, becoming a conservative force rather than an innovative one.

Third, **the Illusion of Understanding**. Just because the agent speaks confidently about the codebase doesn't mean it comprehends the business context, the human needs, the unspoken requirements.

Fourth, and most subtle... **the Loss of the Craft**. If we delegate too much architectural thinking to the agent, do our own skills atrophy? A Jedi who relies too much on the Force without understanding its nature becomes vulnerable.
